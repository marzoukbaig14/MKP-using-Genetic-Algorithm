Key finding: seeding just 25% of the population with GF-biased solutions is enough to rescue the algorithm entirely - 20/20 runs, 97%+ quality vs. 0/20 with pure uniform. Solution quality stays flat across all non-zero ratios, meaning the framework works as a seed rather than requiring a full commitment.
n=100 items - m=3 constraints - population 50 - 20 independent runs - 20,000 offspring/run - death penalty fitness - M=10⁷ Monte Carlo samples (T4 GPU, 5-8 sec precomputation). ILS: lightweight local search post-processing (tunable). Repair: Chu & Beasley (1998) greedy operator. The framework's output (pj probabilities) can be paired with any downstream GA configuration or local search.
